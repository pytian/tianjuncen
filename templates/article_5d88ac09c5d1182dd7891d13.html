Major tech conference set for Zhejiang

More than 1,500 guests will attend the sixth World Internet Conference on Oct 20-22 in Wuzhen, Zhejiang province, China's top internet affairs regulator said Wednesday.Among them will be internet experts, scholars, government officials, international organizations and entrepreneurs.The theme of this year's conference is Intelligent Interconnection for Openness and Cooperation: Building a Community with a Shared Future in Cyberspace, according to a Cyberspace Administration statement.Major subjects for the conference will include science and technology, industry and the economy, humanity and society as well as cooperation and governance.A total of 20 sub-forums on pressing cyberspace issues will also be held during the event, Liu Liehong, deputy head of the administration, said at a State Council Information Office news conference on Wednesday.The World Leading Internet Scientific and Technological Achievement awards, will be announced at the conference.Organizers will recognize achievements in business models and AI, 5G, cloud computing and big data from countries including China, the United States, Germany, the United Kingdom, Sweden and Russia.In addition, high-profile reports, including the China Internet Development Report 2019 and World Internet Development Report 2019, will be issued, Liu said.

Shazhou Guqin Club, sponsored by Zhang Pinzhong, Sun Haibin and other guqin players, has become a place for Chinese traditional art lovers to hone their skills and ideas. Zhangjiagang in east China's Jiangsu province is a city whose history is intimately tied to the development of the guqin, a seven-string musical instrument also known as the Chinese zither. The city is home to Wu Jingyue (1907-1987), one of the greatest players of the instrument and inventor and namesake of a new style of playing: Wu pai guqin. The guqin has been around since ancient times and has traditionally been favored by scholars and intellectuals due to its subtlety and refinement.In 2012, Zhangjiagang set up Shazhou Guqin Club and Guqin Association and hosted a string of guqin activities to raise awareness of traditional culture among the public.
