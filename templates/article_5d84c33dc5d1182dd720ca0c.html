At least 36 people missing in sinking boat in DRC: Police

KINSHASA -- At least 36 people are missing after the sinking of a boat on Sunday at Maluku commune, a district on the banks of the Congo River in Kinshasa's suburbs, according to announces of the Congolese national police on the spot.According to police authorities, only 76 survivors have so far been counted on a hundred of passengers embarked on the edge of this boat which came from the province of Mai-Ndombe, province located southwest of the city of Kinshasa.The boat filled with goods and passengers capsized at Mambutuka locality in Maluku commune. For the moment, research is under way to try to find other survivors in the accident area.No bodies have yet been retrieved by the local river services in the area where the authorities say they have deployed the Congolese army's naval forces, to intensify research on the extent of the accident, which is not away from the main port of the village of Maluku located about fifty kilometers from the city of Kinshasa.Shipwrecks are frequent between the city of Kinshasa and the province of Mai-Ndombe, where dozens of boats leave with passengers to go to refuel agricultural goods and others.The overload and poor conditions of the ships are generally at the root of most shipwrecks on the river and across the country, where most of these ships can not withstand storms during navigation.

At least 50 dead in DRC train derailment

KINSHASA - At least 50 people died early Thursday morning after a freight train derailed in the southeastern province of Tanganyika, Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC), local authorities said.The tragedy occurred around 3 am not far from Mayibaridi locality where the train filled with goods derailed, according to Mulange Mpundu Natacha, the provincial minister of humanitarian actions and solidarity.In Kinshasa, Minister for Humanitarian Affairs Steve Mbikayi also confirmed the information and indicated that an emergency meeting is underway to make the necessary arrangements to assist the victims.Much of DRC's rail network dates back to the colonial era and has had little maintenance. A freight train derailed in Kasai province this March, killing 32 people and wounding 91 others.
