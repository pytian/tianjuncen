Neymar looms large as PSG renews Euro offensive

PARIS - Real Madrid's visit to Paris Saint-Germain on Wednesday is arguably the blockbuster matchup of the opening Champions League round, but one that will bring back unhappy memories for the host.When the clubs last met in the competition's last 16 two seasons ago, a Neymar-less PSG lost 2-1 at Parc des Princes to crash out 5-2 on aggregate, with Cristiano Ronaldo scoring three times over both legs.That was one of three consecutive exits in the first knockout round for PSG, which has failed to make its mark on the Champions League despite the hundreds of millions of euros spent by its Qatari owners.

Unheralded 'little' Dominican squad relishing cutting rivals down to size

SHANGHAI - The Dominican Republic, tied with the Philippines as the shortest team at the FIBA World Cup, is proving that size isn't everything after winning its first two games.The Caribbean nation surged into the second round of the tournament on Tuesday with a gutsy 70-68 victory over favored Germany, to go with an opening 80-76 win over Jordan."Maybe for a lot of people we are the tournament surprise," said the Dominicans' Argentine coach Nestor Garcia after seeing off the Germans. "We believe in ourselves and now we enjoy this moment."But this is only one game, this team can do more things in the World Cup."Their place in the next round assured, the 54-year-old Garcia's men are now sizing up France on Thursday with Group G supremacy at stake.The Dominicans' tallest player is the 6-foot-11 center Eloy Vargas and the average height of their roster is 6-foot-4, according to FIBA statistics.That makes them, along with the Philippines - twice heavy losers in the tournament - the shortest team of the 32 in the competition.The tallest team is Serbia, which averages 6-foot-9.The Dominicans will again be staring upwards at their opponents when they face France (average height 6-foot-8), but forward Ronald Roberts said what they lose in height they make up for in other ways."We played with a lot of energy. They couldn't match the energy the whole game and that was the biggest thing," said Roberts, who plays club basketball in France, after the Germany victory in Shenzhen."We needed to bring the energy. We're the underdogs. We're a little bit smaller than these other teams, but we play harder and that's what's going to keep us going."Stars absent There are a record 54 NBA players at the World Cup - but none represent the Dominican Republic because Garcia is without Karl-Anthony Towns of the Minnesota Timberwolves and the Philadelphia 76ers' Al Horford.That makes the job done by Garcia - a veteran of nearly 30 years' coaching in South America and latterly Spain - all the more commendable.He saw the funny side following the win over Germany when, in the absence of a Spanish translator in Shenzhen, he acted as a stand-in to interpret the comments of star performer Victor Liz, who finished with 17 points, five rebounds and four assists."I did my job, I don't want to be a translator, I've already finished my job," joked Garcia.But taking a more serious turn, he gave an insight into the way that he handles his men when they are facing adversity - and inevitably taller opponents."The world has changed," he said, sounding more philosopher than coach."Before, in the past, if you see, you believe. That was my father's school of thinking."Now, if you believe, you see."Agence France - Presse

China, Argentina vow to advance bilateral ties

BUENOS AIRES - China and Argentina on Wednesday pledged to work together to promote bilateral ties and deepen cooperation.During a meeting here with Argentine President Mauricio Macri, visiting Chinese State Councilor and Minister of National Defense Wei Fenghe conveyed Chinese President Xi Jinping's kind greetings and good wishes to Macri.Macri expressed his gratitude and asked Wei to convey his high respect and sincere regards to Xi, saying China is Argentina's important partner for cooperation, and Argentina appreciates China's long-term help and support.Argentina is committed to strengthening the comprehensive strategic partnership with China and is willing to further deepen bilateral practical cooperation in various fields so as to promote the healthy and stable development of bilateral relations, he added.For his part, Wei said China and Argentina are good friends and good partners.Under the leadership of the two heads of state, China and Argentina maintain mutual political trust, close economic and trade cooperation, support each other on issues concerning their respective core interests, and keep good coordination and communication in international and regional affairs, said Wei.Military relations between the two countries have maintained good momentum and cooperation in various fields has achieved remarkable results, he said.China is ready to work with Argentina to implement the important consensus of the two heads of state and lift their military relations to a higher level, he added.Earlier in the day, Wei held talks with Argentine Defense Minister Oscar Aguad.
